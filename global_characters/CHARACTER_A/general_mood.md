# CHARACTER_A — General Mood

> **Current emotional headspace — for the story period covered by this project.**

Adèle is three months out of a five-year relationship. She's not heartbroken in the dramatic sense — she's in the quieter, harder phase after that: the phase where you have to build a new daily life from scratch and prove to yourself that the leaving was worth it.

She's oscillating between relief and doubt. Some days she feels lighter than she has in years. Other days — usually Sunday evenings — she sits in her new apartment and wonders if she made a mistake. Not because she misses Marc specifically, but because she misses the structure of being known by someone.

Her work is the anchor. The abandoned buildings project gives her purpose and direction. When she's shooting, she doesn't think about any of it. The problem is the hours between.

She's more open than usual — the breakup cracked something in her composure. She's saying things she normally wouldn't. She's surprised by how much she wants to connect with people, after years of telling herself she didn't need to. This openness is fragile and new.
