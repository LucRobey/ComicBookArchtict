## Backstory

**Origin:**
Middle-class family. Father was an electrician, mother a school librarian. One younger sister (Adèle, 29). Grew up trying to escape the Lyon flat, seeking out taller trees and faster bikes in Ardèche. The loud, adventurous sibling who was always scraped up.

**Formative events:**
1) At 16, ran away to Marseille for a week just to see the docks. Got brought back by police but realized the world was much larger than Lyon. 2) At 21, bought a one-way ticket to Colombia. Spent two years working odd jobs and taking photos of street protests. 3) At 26, survived a narrow escape from a flash flood in a remote valley while shooting a documentary, which cemented his belief in his own invincibility.

**Turning points:**
The failure of his last field project three months ago. A contact got arrested, and Thomas had to leave behind his equipment and files to escape. It was the first time he felt genuinely out of his depth.

**Current situation:**
Living day-to-day. Working as a stringer for international news agencies, but refusing steady desk work. Trying to rebuild a relationship with his sister Adèle, who he worries about but doesn't know how to comfort without being overbearing.

## Psychology

**Core driver:**
To be where the action is, to live at the absolute limit of his senses. He wants to feel alive, which he only does when there's something at stake.

**Core fear:**
Being trapped in a predictable, domestic routine. He's terrified of waking up one day and realizing he has a mortgage, a 9-to-5 job, and nothing to tell stories about.

**Self-image:**
The free agent. A modern-day explorer who can handle any situation and doesn't owe anyone anything. He prides himself on his quick reflexes and adaptability.

**The gap:**
He uses chaos as an escape from intimacy. By keeping his life constantly unstable, he avoids having to commit to anyone or face his own internal emptiness.

**Default coping mechanism:**
Joking, deflecting, or leaving. If an emotional conversation starts, he'll crack a joke, change the subject, or suddenly remember an "urgent appointment."

**What they're proud of:**
His reputation for getting the shot in difficult conditions. He's known for being reliable in a crisis.

**What they're ashamed of:**
Having abandoned a local fixer during a hasty exit from a conflict zone two years ago, even though there was nothing else he could have done. It haunts him when he can't sleep.
